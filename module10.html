Fantasy

In this module, we will focus on fantasy books and how they allow students to use their imagination and understand symbolism and motifs through reading.

  • What is fantasy?
  • What are some of the special elements that classify a fantasy story?
  • What are some themes that drive fantasy?
  • What are different types of fantasies?
  • Why is teaching fantasy beneficial to students?
  • What is the difference between fantasy and other genres?

Through reading, writing , and particapting as a class we will explore these questions.

The Tasks

Read

Complete the readings.

Annotate one of the readings.

Write-Complete Both

Write an open-ended reflection about Fantasy fiction. Some questions to guide your reflection: (optional)

Have you ever read any Fantasy novels? If so, what were they? Why did you like/dislike them?

What are some of common themes you have seen in Fantasy books before?

What is your favorite type of Fantasy? Why?

Do you think using Fantasy in the classroom is beneficial for students?

What are some elements of Fantasy that you enjoy?

Participate

Your task is to create an interesting magical creature that includes a short description of how this creature came to be. What does this creature look like? What does/can it do? Does it have any special powers? You can do this through pictures or word description. Have fun with it and be creative!
