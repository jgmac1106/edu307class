Fantasy

In this module, we will focus on fantasy books and how they allow students to use their imagination and understand symbolism and motifs through reading.

  • How do fantasy books create a different perspective?
  • How can students relate to motifs through fantasy books?
  • How can you incorporate creativity through this mod?

Through reading, writing , and particapting as a class we will explore these questions.

The Readings

Robin Fuxa Finding Fantasy: The Genre that Makes Difficult Topics Easier for Students to Discuss

Rita SoltanFairy Tale Characters Breathe New Life: A Fantasy Book Club Approach for Tweens

The Tasks

Read

Complete the readings.

Annotate one of the readings.

Write-Complete Both

Do you think that fantasy and science fiction books, like Harry Potter, The Hunger Games and the hundreds of age appropriate books and series, should be a part of standard curriculum or read as optional pleasure/summer reading books?

Would you assign and teach fantasy and sci-fi books in your middle school/ high school classroom? If yes, explain why and which book(s) would you assign and to what grade? If no, explain why and what type of books would you focus on instead?

Participate

Students will imagine their own fantasy world, and then, portray it visually. They may draw or paint their world on paper or craft together a diorama out of toilet paper rolls and legos, or create collage. Pictures are welcomed, building a blanket fort with lights and different colors. In one to two sentences, explain your creation.
