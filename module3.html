Overview Show

In this module we we will we take a critical look at children's literature. Specifically we will understand how voices can be silenced or hidden in curriculum. More importantly we will explore how to use literature as tool for trajectory. A source of agency

  • What does deficit thinking do to the literature classroom?
  • How can we use specific texts as tools tools for teaching and empowerment?
  • What are some great stories to share?

Through reading, writing , and particapting as a class we will explore these questions.

The Tasks

Read

As a note to all: We will read sensitive materials during this module. We are asking questions that stir emotions and memories. They may challenge deeply held beliefs and societal structures. No one is ever required to publish their thoughts at any time. If you ever need an alternative assignment please ask. When we are gathering as a class lets be open to all perspectives during our discussions.

Most importantly remember to listen.

Complete the readings.

Choose two out of four to annotate. The annotation tool we will use as a class is installed but you can annotate any way you choose.

Write

Write a blog post about your reactions to the reading.

Look for two to three examples of complex literacy practices not commonly recognized in the classrooms. write about it

Participate

Performance Task: You will start your personal pathway in class. These are long term sassignments you should start./p>

We will all be choosing texts from The Collaborative for Equity In Literacy Learning. Your path will diverge here based on choice

Reading Pathway:
Choose two books from the list. Get together and read them in a group. Organize a snapchat or instagram meet up (sharingq pics of quotes and thoughts, meet face to face for coffee, use text messages. Use the class stream. Just read and discuss the books together. Publish

Writing Pathway
Choose one of the genres. Pick 3-5 of the summaries. Annotate the text to identify text structure. Your group of writers should produce a minimum of three short stories that address issues of diversity.
