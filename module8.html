Fables

In this module we will explore the use of fables in the classroom. Fables are a great tool to teach morals to children and be used to expand their knowledge of reading and writing.

  • What are some ways in which fables can be used in relation to Common Core?
  • Through reading fables what are some ways we can use common elements when we teach writing?
  • Why are fables important to teach?

Through reading, writing , and particapting as a class we will explore these questions.

The Tasks

Read

Complete the readings.

Annotate one of the readings.

Write-Choose One

After reading several of Aesop’s fables, try your hand at writing a fable of your own, using animal characters in the starring roles and leaving a moral to be learned at the end.

After reading several of Aesop’s fables, choose your favorite fable and rewrite the ending so that it ends up teaching the reader a new lesson or so that it has a different moral to the story.

Participate

Read The Fox and the Grapes. Then as a class and discuss the moral of the story and analyze the fable story elements.Post a few questions to the stream you could ask students that would have them look at the the moral.
